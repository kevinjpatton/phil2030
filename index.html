Welcome to
Intro to Ethics.

An exploration of moral values, moral theories, and the nature of obligation.

Course Overview

What makes an action right or wrong? How should we construct a good life? This course surveys the major normative ethical theories—including Utilitarianism, Kantian Deontology, and Virtue Ethics—and challenges students to apply these frameworks to complex, real-world problems.

Required Texts

There is no textbook to purchase for this class. We will be using my Ethics: A New Introduction book. All other primary source readings will be provided as free PDFs on this site under the Schedule tab.

Classroom Culture

Ethics involves sensitive topics. We will practice charitable reading, respectful disagreement, and open-minded inquiry. Attack the argument, not the person.

Double Major in Philosophy

The benefits of adding a philosophy major to your degree are immense. We specialize in critically assessing arguments, ideas, and claims of truth. If you care about that, then you should become a double major.
